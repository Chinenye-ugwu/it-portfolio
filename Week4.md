## Ethical Dilemma Alternative and Reflection

### Ethical Dilemma

In Week 2, I reflected on a situation in which an insurance manager recorded and posted a video of a life insurance beneficiary visiting the office without first obtaining the beneficiary's clear consent. Although the manager may have intended to promote the company's services or celebrate a successful claim, the action raised significant ethical concerns regarding privacy and the responsible handling of personal information.

### Essential Ethical Principles

Several ethical principles are essential in this situation:

- **Respect for Privacy:** Individuals have the right to control how their personal information and images are collected, used, and shared.
- **Informed Consent:** Organizations should obtain explicit permission before recording or publishing identifiable information about clients.
- **Integrity:** IT and business professionals should act honestly and responsibly, even when no one is watching.
- **Professional Responsibility:** Employees have a duty to protect confidential information and maintain public trust.
- **Respect and Dignity:** Clients should always be treated with respect, especially during sensitive situations such as insurance claims.

These principles align with the ACM Code of Ethics, which emphasizes respecting privacy, avoiding harm, maintaining honesty, and protecting confidential information.

### Alternative Approach

A more ethical approach would have been to ask the beneficiary for written or recorded consent before taking any photographs or videos. The manager should clearly explain why the recording is being requested, how it will be used, where it may be published, and assure the client that declining will not affect the services they receive.

If the beneficiary chooses not to give consent, that decision should be respected without question. The company could instead promote its services by using anonymous success stories, employee interviews, or educational content that does not reveal a client's identity or personal circumstances.

Additionally, the organization should establish a formal media consent policy and provide regular staff training on privacy, confidentiality, and ethical communication. These measures would help ensure that all employees understand their responsibilities when handling client information.

### Why This Alternative Is Better

This alternative protects the beneficiary's privacy while preserving trust between clients and the insurance company. Clients are more likely to feel respected and secure when they know their personal information will only be shared with their permission.

Obtaining informed consent also reduces legal and reputational risks for the organization by demonstrating compliance with ethical and professional standards. A clear consent process promotes transparency and accountability, strengthening the organization's credibility.

As someone preparing for a career in Information Technology and System Administration, this situation reinforces the importance of protecting sensitive information. Whether managing cloud systems, user accounts, or customer data, I recognize that technical knowledge must always be guided by ethical decision-making. Technology professionals have a responsibility to safeguard information, respect individual privacy, and build systems that promote trust and accountability.

This experience has strengthened my understanding that ethical leadership is not only about following policies but also about making decisions that place respect for people at the center of every professional action.
