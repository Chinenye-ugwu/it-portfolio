## Introduction

Week 1 marked the beginning of my IT 497 Capstone journey. It gave me the opportunity to reflect on my academic progress, career transition, spiritual development, and professional goals as I prepare for a career in Information Technology.

During this week, I began building both my Spiritual Growth Portfolio and Professional Growth Portfolio. These portfolios will continue to grow throughout the capstone course and serve as evidence of my technical development, professional readiness, and personal values.

---

## SMART Goals

One of the first activities in Week 1 was creating SMART goals that will guide my development throughout IT 497.

### Goal 1 – Strengthen My Technical Skills

Continue developing practical skills in Linux administration, Windows Server, Microsoft Azure, Amazon Web Services (AWS), networking, cybersecurity, and systems administration through coursework, hands-on projects, and independent learning.

### Goal 2 – Build a Professional Portfolio

Develop a professional portfolio that documents my academic projects, certifications, technical reports, résumé, and reflective learning throughout my System Administration program.

### Goal 3 – Successfully Transition into Information Technology

Prepare for an entry-level career in System Administration or Cloud Support by combining my technical education with more than ten years of leadership and customer service experience gained in the insurance industry.

These goals provide a clear roadmap for my continued academic, professional, and personal development throughout the capstone course.

## Spiritual Growth Portfolio

Throughout my academic journey, I have come to understand that professional success requires more than technical knowledge alone. Developing strong character, exercising faith, and striving to serve others are equally important. As I have progressed through my System Administration program, I have learned that spiritual principles have strengthened my ability to overcome challenges, remain committed to lifelong learning, and prepare for a career built on integrity and service.

### Faith in Jesus Christ

Faith has been the foundation of my educational journey. Entering the System Administration program without a technical background was intimidating, and there were times when learning Linux, networking, Windows Server, cloud computing, and cybersecurity felt overwhelming.

My faith has reminded me that education is not only about gaining technical knowledge but also about developing character, discipline, and perseverance. Rather than becoming discouraged by difficult assignments, I learned to approach challenges with patience and determination. Looking back, I recognize that my progress has been made possible through hard work, persistence, and trust in God.

**Professional Application**

As I prepare for a career in Information Technology, I want to continue relying on faith when facing new technologies, workplace challenges, and opportunities for growth. Faith gives me confidence to continue learning and solving difficult technical problems.

### Integrity

Integrity has become increasingly important as I have studied Information Technology. System administrators are entrusted with protecting sensitive information, maintaining secure systems, and supporting organizational operations. These responsibilities require honesty, accountability, and ethical decision-making.

Throughout my coursework, I have learned that technical ability must always be accompanied by personal integrity. Whether completing assignments, documenting my work accurately, or supporting future users and organizations, I want my actions to reflect honesty and professionalism.

**Professional Application**

As an IT professional, I want to build trust by handling information responsibly, respecting confidentiality, following organizational policies, and demonstrating ethical behavior in every task I perform.

### Lifelong Learning and Service

One of the greatest lessons I have learned during my studies is that education does not end with graduation. Technology changes rapidly, making continuous learning essential for every Information Technology professional.

I have also developed a greater appreciation for serving others. Throughout my studies, I have enjoyed helping classmates when possible and sharing ideas that encourage learning and collaboration. I hope to continue using my knowledge and skills to support others, solve problems, and make a positive contribution wherever I work.

**Professional Application**

I am committed to continually developing my technical skills through professional certifications, hands-on experience, and ongoing education. At the same time, I want to use those skills to serve others with patience, professionalism, and a willingness to help.
