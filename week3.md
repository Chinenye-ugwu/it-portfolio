## Guiding Principles

During Week 3, I identified three guiding principles that will continue to shape both my spiritual journey and my professional development as I prepare for a career in Information Technology.

### Seek God's Guidance Through Every Transition

Transitioning from a career of more than ten years in the insurance industry into Information Technology has required faith, patience, and trust in God. Entering a completely new profession meant becoming a beginner again, learning unfamiliar technologies, and stepping outside my comfort zone.

Proverbs 3:5–6 reminds me to trust in the Lord rather than relying solely on my own understanding. This principle encourages me to seek God's guidance as I continue learning Linux, networking, cloud computing, cybersecurity, and system administration.

---

### Learn with Humility and Perseverance

Learning Information Technology has taught me that mistakes are part of the learning process. Every technical challenge has helped me become a better problem solver while reminding me that growth requires patience and persistence.

Although my previous career provided valuable leadership and communication skills, I have embraced the opportunity to become a lifelong learner. Technology continues to evolve, and I want to remain humble enough to keep learning throughout my career.

---

### Serve Others Through Integrity

Technology should always be used to benefit people. My experience in both insurance and Information Technology has reinforced the importance of honesty, confidentiality, and professional responsibility.

Whether protecting sensitive information, supporting users, or maintaining secure systems, I want my work to reflect integrity, respect, and a genuine desire to serve others.
